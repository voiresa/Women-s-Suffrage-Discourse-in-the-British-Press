# Digital-Humanity-Project
Title:
Women's Suffrage Discourse in the British Press (Pre-1928)

Project Description:
A digital humanities analysis of how British newspapers framed the women's suffrage movement from the late 19th century until the Equal Franchise Act of 1928. This project applies computational methods on data from the Gale Digital Scholar Lab to track the evolution of language, themes, and sentiment in media discourse, deepening understanding of the interactive relationship between public opinions and social movements.
